The History of Chess

The game of Chess originated from the Indian game of chaturanga before 600 AD. However, it didn't begin to evolve into the game we know now until the mid 18th century. During that time French Master Francois-Andre Philidor published a book introducing theories of defense that are still used today. Chess continued to grow in popularity, then in the 19th century chess sets became standardized. Beyond this point the game of chess itself did not change, nevertheless, players approach to winning did. Early chess players had very attack heavy strategies, because defensive play styles hadn't been learned yet. That is until Wilhelm Steinitz who became the first world chess champion using his defensive style of play. Strategies continue to advance even now, current chess champions use computerized chess engines in order to improve their skills.

Chess Piece Rules

  • Pawns can move one square straight ahead
  • Rooks can move in their line or row
  • Knights can move two squares vertically and one square horizontally or vise versa
  • Bishops can move diagonally
  • The King can move from their square to a neighboring square
  • The Queen can move like a Rook or a Bishop
